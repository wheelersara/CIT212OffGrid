Land, Shelter and Food

You will need to select a location that includes: Access to water, decent climate, good soil quality, sun exposure, access to town, and meets legal requirements.

You should also consider the type of shelter that you will live in. Some options include: cabins, traditional wood-frame homes, tiny houses, cob houses, Earthships, strawbale homes, or yurts.

You should make sure that you have a plentiful amount of clean accessable water from a well, rain water collection, or other natural water source.

Making sure to grow your own food in a garden and preserving it will ensure you have food all year round.

Power Sources

You will need to choose a way to power your shelter by using a system such as sola panels or wind turbines.

Alternate sources of energy are highly encouraged, such as gas or bio-diesel-powered generators.

Battery backups are strongly reccomended in situations where natural power sources are not sustainable

Choosing a heating and cooling system varies on location and climate, but wood stoves are good for heating and cooking, solar fans are good for cooling. Make sure your shelter is insulated!

Waste

You need a method for removing waste. Composting waste disposal system or a traditional septic system are good contenders.

It is important to be mindful of kitchen waste and trash management as well. A good way to manage this is by composting.

The Off-Grid Mindset

Having a realistic, self motivated mindset is very important for living off grid

Adopting a sustainable lifestyle requires strong commitment, clear focus, and motivation.
